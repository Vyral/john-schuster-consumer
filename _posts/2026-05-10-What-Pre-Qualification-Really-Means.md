[**Get pre-qualified now.**](https://granitebank.com/loan-officer/cliftonkrotz/)<br /><br />Before you fall in love with a house online, there’s one thing you need to do first.

[Clifton Krotz](https://www.johnschustergroup.com/financing), our preferred lender at Granite Bank, says one of the most common mistakes he sees buyers make is shopping before they truly understand their numbers. Here’s what that means in practice.

**Pre-qualification is more than a price range.** Most buyers think getting pre-qualified means finding out the maximum amount they can spend. That’s part of it, but it misses what actually matters. The number you need to know is your actual monthly payment, including principal, interest, property taxes, homeowners’ insurance, and HOA fees, if applicable.

A $400,000 home in one Twin Cities neighborhood can carry a very different monthly cost than a $400,000 home in another, depending on the tax rate and whether there’s an association. If you’re only looking at the sticker price, you’re working with incomplete information.

{% include pullquote.html %}

**How you buy matters as much as what you buy.** In today’s market, sellers aren’t just looking for the highest offer. They want certainty, clean financing, realistic timelines, and minimal risk that the deal will fall apart. Buyers who have done the work upfront, who know exactly what they can afford and how their financing is structured, are the ones writing offers that sellers take seriously. If your offer comes in with shaky pre-approval or unrealistic contingencies, you’re already behind the buyer who showed up prepared.

**If you need to sell before you buy, there are options.** The timing problem is one of the biggest obstacles for move-up buyers: you need the equity from your current home to buy the next one, but you don’t want to sell first and end up without a place to live. Cash to Win is a program through Granite Bank that lets buyers shop and even make a cash-style offer on a new home without waiting to sell their current property first. It changes the math entirely for buyers who feel stuck between two transactions.

**The right plan saves time, stress, and money.** Buyers who sort this out before they start scrolling end up in a stronger position at every stage of the process. They know their numbers. They know how to structure an offer that stands out. Buyers who skip this step find out the hard way.

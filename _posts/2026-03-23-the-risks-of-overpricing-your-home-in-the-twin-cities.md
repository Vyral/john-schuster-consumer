Ever thought about listing your home high just to see what happens? It might seem harmless, but that approach can actually hurt your chances of getting strong offers early on.

Here’s why that first decision matters more than most sellers realize.

The truth is, buyers react instantly to pricing signals when a home is first listed. That first window gets the most attention, the strongest offers, and the most emotion from buyers.

If you miss that initial window, every price change afterward can look reactive instead of strategic. The strongest leverage you have as a seller happens right at launch.

**The solution is simple: price your home correctly from the beginning.** When you do, buyers will compete for your home. Miss the price, and buyers may just wait. If you want control over your sale, the first price matters most.
